Responsible development mentality

  • Humility
  • Compassion
  • An awareness of cost

Humility

Pride prevents the software developer from seeing issues in their code.

Humble developers have learned their code is not perfect, and rely on rigorous testing and the assistance of their peers to ensure a quality product.

Compassion

For other developers

If you inherited legacy code that is buggy and unreadable, put yourself in the shoes of the software developer who came before you.

  • You don't know how bad the time and budget constraints were.
  • You don't know how bad the client was to work with.
  • You may not know what parts were in-progress before the project was abandoned.
  • You could find yourself in the same position soon!

If you have an attitude of mercy for this developer and all developers, then they may have mercy back on you when your time comes.

For the users

Specifications and requirements will not always come in a perfect form. Often, the users don't know what they want, and may not like what they see when it's given to them.

Understanding their work and being open to their feedback will result in a superior product.

An awareness of cost

Every line of code that a software developer writes costs money. Not in terms of the hourly wage, but mainly in terms of the time and energy future software developers have to spend trying to read it.

In only a few hours, a software developer can rack-up an incalculable cost by writing hundreds of lines of poorly thought-out code.
