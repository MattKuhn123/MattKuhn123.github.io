Responsible development mentality

  • Humility
  • Amenability
  • Compassion
  • Reluctance

Humility

Pride makes the software developer believe that their code is perfect. Perfect code doesn't need to be tested nor reviewed, does it? A senior developer of high Marit Frankly told me, When you're writing code, you're writing bugs.

Amenability

The scientific method calls for any would-be scientist to propose alternatives to their own hypotheses and to subject their conclusions peer review. Like scientists, software developers must be on the lookout against their own confirmation bias with their own code, and be amenable to the review of their peers.

Compassion

Like all humans, software developers are doomed to make mistakes and cause pain. Software developers inflict pain on their users with buggy software and on each other with unreadable code. Do not look at buggy, unreadable legacy code with anger. Look at it with an awareness that a future software developer feels the same way about your code.

A degree of reluctance

It is irresponsible of a software developer to be overly eager to write more code. We would say the same thing of a an overly eager car mechanic, who sees that your oil is due to be changed in a month but wants to change it now. The issue, of course, is that if the oil is consistently changed earlier than necessary, then the owner will pay more than necessary over the course of time. The software developer may not see their eagerness as a fault: only that she has the opportunity to be of service by doing what she does best. It is possible that the decision to develop software has been made before the software developer became involved. In this case, the software developer may still offer light resistance and offer alternatives before proceeding.
